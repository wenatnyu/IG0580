Part 1: Key Concepts

1. Scale Ratio (\(1:n\)): Represents the relationship between the drawing and real life.

  • \( 1 : n \) means 1 unit on the map = n units in reality.
  • Important: Units must be consistent before writing the ratio! (e.g., both in cm).

2. Unit Conversions (Memorize):

  • 1 km = 1000 m = 100,000 cm
  • 1 m = 100 cm

3. Area Scale Factor (Advanced): If the linear scale is \( 1:n \), the area scale is \( 1 : n^2 \).

Part 2: In-Class Examples

Ex 1. A map has a scale of \( 1 : 200,000 \). The distance between two towns on the map is 4.5 cm. Calculate the actual distance in km.
\( 4.5 \times 200,000 = 900,000 \text{ cm} \). Divide by 100,000 = 9 km.
Ex 2. A real soccer pitch is 100m long. On a drawing, it is 20cm long. Find the scale of the drawing in the form \( 1:n \).
100m = 10,000cm. Ratio \( 20 : 10,000 \). Divide by 20 \( \rightarrow \) 1 : 500.

Part 3: Homework Practice
Chapter 15.1
Q1. (Basic) A map has a scale of \( 1 : 50,000 \). Calculate the actual distance, in kilometers, represented by a length of 8 cm on the map.
\( 8 \times 50,000 = 400,000 \text{ cm} = 4 \text{ km} \).
Q2. (Reverse) The actual distance between Paris and London is approximately 340 km. Calculate the distance between these two cities on a map with a scale of \( 1 : 10,000,000 \). Give your answer in centimeters.
\( 340 \text{ km} = 34,000,000 \text{ cm} \).
\( 34,000,000 \div 10,000,000 = 3.4 \text{ cm} \).
Q3. (Finding Scale) A model of a building is 15 cm tall. The actual building is 30 meters tall. Write the scale of the model in the form \( 1 : n \).
\( 30 \text{ m} = 3000 \text{ cm} \). Ratio \( 15 : 3000 \). Divide by 15 \( \rightarrow \) 1 : 200.
Q4. (Area Problem) A map has a scale of \( 1 : 20,000 \). A lake on the map has an area of \( 5 \text{ cm}^2 \). Calculate the actual area of the lake in square kilometers (\( \text{km}^2 \)).
Hint: Convert the scale to \( 1 \text{ cm} = \text{?} \text{ km} \) first, then square it.
Method 1: Linear scale \( 1 \text{ cm} = 0.2 \text{ km} \).
Area scale = \( (0.2)^2 = 0.04 \text{ km}^2 \) per \( \text{cm}^2 \).
Total Area = \( 5 \times 0.04 = 0.2 km}^2 \).
Q5. (Challenge) A rectangular swimming pool measures 25m by 12m. On a scale drawing, the longer side is 10 cm.
(a) Find the scale.
(b) Calculate the width of the pool on the drawing.
(a) \( 25 \text{ m} = 2500 \text{ cm} \). Ratio \( 10:2500 = 1:250 \).
(b) Width \( 12 \text{ m} = 1200 \text{ cm} \). \( 1200 \div 250 = 4.8 \text{ cm} \).
