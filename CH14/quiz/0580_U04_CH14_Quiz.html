1. Solve the simultaneous equations. (Topic 14.1)
$$ \begin{align*} 2x + 3y &= 16 \\ 5x - 2y &= 21 \end{align*} $$
Answer: \( x = 5, y = 2 \)
Method: Multiply top by 2, bottom by 3 to eliminate y, or use substitution.
2. Two sandwiches and three soft drinks cost $18. Five sandwiches and two soft drinks cost $28.50. Calculate the cost of one sandwich and one soft drink. (Topic 14.1)
Answer: Sandwich = $4.50, Drink = $3.00
Let x be sandwich, y be drink. Solve \(2x+3y=18\) and \(5x+2y=28.5\).
3. Solve the inequality. (Topic 14.2)
$$ 3(x - 2) < 5x + 4 $$
Answer: \( x > -5 \)
Steps: \(3x - 6 < 5x + 4 \Rightarrow -10 < 2x \Rightarrow -5 < x\)
4. Find the integer values of \( n \) that satisfy the inequality. (Topic 14.2)
$$ -7 \le 2n - 3 < 5 $$
Answer: \( n = -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3 \)
Steps: Add 3 to all parts: \(-4 \le 2n < 8\). Divide by 2: \(-2 \le n < 4\).
5. Solve the inequality and represent the solution on a number line (sketch the line below). (Topic 14.2)
$$ \frac{4-x}{3} \ge 2 $$
Answer: \( x \le -2 \)
Multiply by 3: \(4 - x \ge 6\). Subtract 4: \(-x \ge 2\). Divide by -1 (flip sign): \(x \le -2\).
6. Write down the three inequalities that define the unshaded region R, where the boundaries are the x-axis, the y-axis, and a line passing through (0, 4) and (4, 0). (Topic 14.3)
Answer: \( x \ge 0 \), \( y \ge 0 \), \( x + y \le 4 \) (or \( y \le -x + 4 \))
7. Find the point with integer coordinates \((x, y)\) that lies inside the region defined by \(y > x\), \(y < 4\) and \(x > 1\). (Topic 14.3)
Answer: \( (2, 3) \)
Since x must be > 1, x can be 2. If x=2, y must be > 2 and < 4. So y=3.
8. Write \( x^2 + 6x - 7 \) in the form \( (x+a)^2 + b \). (Topic 14.4)
Answer: \( (x+3)^2 - 16 \)
Half of 6 is 3. \( (x+3)^2 = x^2 + 6x + 9 \). Subtract 9 and original 7: \(-9 - 7 = -16\).
9. Solve the equation \( x^2 - 8x + 5 = 0 \) by completing the square. Give your answers in exact form (using surds). (Topic 14.4)
Answer: \( x = 4 \pm \sqrt{11} \)
\( (x-4)^2 - 16 + 5 = 0 \Rightarrow (x-4)^2 = 11 \Rightarrow x - 4 = \pm\sqrt{11} \)
10. Find the coordinates of the minimum point of the graph \( y = x^2 + 10x + 30 \) by completing the square. (Topic 14.4)
Answer: Minimum at \( (-5, 5) \)
Equation becomes \( y = (x+5)^2 + 5 \).
11. Solve \( 2x^2 + 5x - 4 = 0 \) using the quadratic formula. Show all working and give your answers correct to 2 decimal places. (Topic 14.5)
Answer: \( x = 0.64 \) or \( x = -3.14 \)
Formula: \( x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \). \( x = \frac{-5 \pm \sqrt{25 - 4(2)(-4)}}{4} = \frac{-5 \pm \sqrt{57}}{4} \)
12. A rectangle has a length of \( (x+3) \) cm and a width of \( x \) cm. The area of the rectangle is 15 cm². Form an equation and solve it to find the value of \( x \) correct to 3 significant figures. (Topic 14.5)
Answer: \( x = 2.68 \)
Eq: \( x(x+3) = 15 \Rightarrow x^2 + 3x - 15 = 0 \). Use quadratic formula. Positive x only.
13. Factorise completely: \( 2x^2 + 7x + 3 \). (Topic 14.6)
Answer: \( (2x + 1)(x + 3) \)
14. Factorise completely: \( 6x^2 - 11x - 10 \). (Topic 14.6)
Answer: \( (3x + 2)(2x - 5) \)
15. Factorise completely: \( 8x^2 - 18 \). (Topic 14.6)
Answer: \( 2(2x - 3)(2x + 3) \)
First take out factor 2: \( 2(4x^2 - 9) \). Then use difference of squares.
16. Simplify the algebraic fraction. (Topic 14.7)
$$ \frac{x^2 - 9}{2x^2 + 6x} $$
Answer: \( \frac{x-3}{2x} \)
Factorise numerator: \((x-3)(x+3)\). Factorise denominator: \(2x(x+3)\). Cancel \((x+3)\).
17. Write as a single fraction in its simplest form. (Topic 14.7)
$$ \frac{3}{x+2} + \frac{4}{x-1} $$
Answer: \( \frac{7x + 5}{(x+2)(x-1)} \)
Num: \( 3(x-1) + 4(x+2) = 3x - 3 + 4x + 8 = 7x + 5 \).
18. Solve the equation. (Topic 14.7)
$$ \frac{x}{3} + \frac{x-2}{5} = 4 $$
Answer: \( x = 6.75 \) (or \( \frac{27}{4} \))
Common denom 15: \( 5x + 3(x-2) = 60 \Rightarrow 8x - 6 = 60 \Rightarrow 8x = 66 \).
19. Solve the equation. (Topic 14.7)
$$ \frac{2}{x-1} - \frac{3}{x+2} = 0 $$
Answer: \( x = 7 \)
Move to sides: \( \frac{2}{x-1} = \frac{3}{x+2} \). Cross multiply: \( 2(x+2) = 3(x-1) \Rightarrow 2x + 4 = 3x - 3 \).
20. (Challenge) Solve for \(x\). Give your answers to 2 decimal places. (Topics 14.5 & 14.7)
$$ x + \frac{4}{x} = 7 $$
Answer: \( x = 6.37 \) or \( x = 0.63 \)
Multiply by x: \( x^2 + 4 = 7x \Rightarrow x^2 - 7x + 4 = 0 \). Use quadratic formula.
