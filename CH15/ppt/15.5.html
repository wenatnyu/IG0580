Part 1: Strategy & Vocabulary

1. Angles of Elevation & Depression:

  • Always measured from the Horizontal Line.
  • Angle of Depression (looking down) is equal to the Angle of Elevation (looking up) from the other point (Alternate Angles / Z-angles).

2. Strategy for Word Problems:

  1. Sketch: Draw a diagram. Label all lengths and angles.
  2. Triangle: Find the right-angled triangle.
  3. Choose: Select SOH, CAH, or TOA.
  4. Solve: Rearrange and calculate.

Part 2: In-Class Examples

Ex 1. (Elevation) A man stands 50m away from the base of a tower. The angle of elevation to the top of the tower is \( 32^\circ \). The man's eyes are 1.8m above the ground. Calculate the total height of the tower.
Triangle part height \( h \): \( \tan 32 = \frac{h}{50} \Rightarrow h = 50 \tan 32 \approx 31.24 \).
Total Height = \( 31.24 + 1.8 = 33.0 m \).
Ex 2. (Bearings Context) Ship A is 10 km due North of a lighthouse. Ship B is 10 km due East of the lighthouse. Calculate the bearing of Ship B from Ship A.
Draw diagram. Isosceles right triangle (10km, 10km).
Angle inside triangle at A is \( \tan^{-1}(10/10) = 45^\circ \).
Bearing is from North line (which is line LA). Bearing = 135¬∞.

Part 3: Homework Practice
Chapter 15.5
Q1. (Basic Elevation) A kite is flying on a string of length 40m. The string makes an angle of \( 55^\circ \) with the horizontal ground. Assuming the string is straight, calculate the height of the kite above the ground.
Hyp = 40, Angle = 55, find Opp.
\( \sin 55 = \frac{h}{40} \Rightarrow h = 40 \sin 55 \approx 32.8 \text{ m} \).
Q2. (Depression) From the top of a cliff 120m high, a boat is observed at an angle of depression of \( 18^\circ \). Calculate how far the boat is from the base of the cliff.
Angle of elevation from boat = 18¬∞. Opp = 120. Find Adj.
\( \tan 18 = \frac{120}{x} \Rightarrow x = \frac{120}{\tan 18} \approx 369.3 \text{ m} \).
Q3. (Isosceles Triangle) An isosceles triangle has two sides of length 15 cm and a base of length 10 cm. Calculate the size of the base angles. (Hint: Split it into two right-angled triangles).
Drop perpendicular. Adj = 5, Hyp = 15.
\( \cos \theta = \frac{5}{15} \Rightarrow \theta = \cos^{-1}(0.333) \approx 70.5^\circ \).
Q4. (Multi-step) Two buildings are 20m apart. From the top of the shorter building (15m high), the angle of elevation to the top of the taller building is \( 25^\circ \). Calculate the height of the taller building.
1. Form triangle at top. Adj = 20, Angle = 25. Find Opp \( x \).
\( \tan 25 = \frac{x}{20} \Rightarrow x = 9.33 \text{ m} \).
2. Total height = \( 15 + 9.33 = 24.33 \text{ m} \).
Q5. (Bearings & Distances) A ship sails 50 km on a bearing of \( 040^\circ \). It then sails due East for another 30 km.
(a) Calculate how far North the ship is from its starting point.
(b) Calculate the total straight-line distance from the start.
(a) North dist = \( 50 \cos 40 = 38.3 \text{ km} \).
(b) East dist 1 = \( 50 \sin 40 = 32.14 \text{ km} \). Total East = \( 32.14 + 30 = 62.14 \).
Distance \( = \sqrt{38.3^2 + 62.14^2} \approx 73.0 \text{ km} \).
