Part 2: In-Class Examples

Ex 1. Evaluate \( 2 \sin 30^\circ \) without a calculator.
\( 2 \times 0.5 = 1 \).
Ex 2. Show that \( \tan 60^\circ \times \cos 60^\circ = \sin 60^\circ \).
LHS: \( \sqrt{3} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \).
RHS: \( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \). (Correct).
Ex 3. Evaluate \( \cos 45^\circ - \sin 30^\circ \) exactly.
\( \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} - \frac{1}{2} \).
Ex 4. Solve for \( x \) in \( 0^\circ \le x \le 90^\circ \) if \( \sin x = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \).
\( x = 60^\circ \).
Ex 5. Show that \( \sin^2 30^\circ + \cos^2 30^\circ = 1 \).
\((1/2)^2 + (\sqrt{3}/2)^2 = 1/4 + 3/4 = 1\).

Part 3: Homework Practice

Q1. (Recall) Write down the exact values of:
(a) \( \tan 45^\circ \)    (b) \( \cos 30^\circ \)    (c) \( \sin 60^\circ \)
(a) 1   (b) \( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \)   (c) \( \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \)
Q2. (Evaluate) Calculate the exact value of \( \sin 45^\circ + \cos 45^\circ \). Write your answer in the form \( \sqrt{k} \).
\( \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} + \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} = \frac{2\sqrt{2}}{2} = \sqrt{2} \).
Q3. (Solve) Find the acute angle \( x \) if \( 2 \cos x = 1 \).
\( \cos x = \frac{1}{2} \). From table, \( x = 60^\circ \).
Q4. (Identity) Show that \( \frac{\sin 30^\circ}{\cos 30^\circ} = \tan 30^\circ \).
\( \frac{1/2}{\sqrt{3}/2} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \), which is \( \tan 30 \).
Q5. (Application) An equilateral triangle has a side length of 2 units. Use trigonometry to show that the height of the triangle is \( \sqrt{3} \).
Split into right-angled triangle. Hyp = 2, Base = 1. Angle = \( 60^\circ \).
\( \sin 60 = \frac{h}{2} \Rightarrow \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = \frac{h}{2} \Rightarrow h = \sqrt{3} \).
