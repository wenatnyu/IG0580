Part 1: Key Concepts

Rules of Bearings:

  1. Measure from North (000¬∞).
  2. Measure Clockwise.
  3. Use 3 Digits (e.g., \( 045^\circ \), \( 230^\circ \)).

Calculations:

  • Back Bearing: "A from B". If original is \( < 180 \), add 180. If \( > 180 \), subtract 180.
  • Co-interior Angles: North lines are parallel. Angles inside sum to \( 180^\circ \).

Part 2: In-Class Examples

Ex 1. The bearing of ship B from ship A is \( 060^\circ \). Calculate the bearing of ship A from ship B.
Add 180: \( 60 + 180 = 240^\circ \).
Ex 2. Point A is due South of Point B. Find the bearing of A from B, and B from A.
A from B (Start at B, look South): 180¬∞.
B from A (Start at A, look North): 000¬∞ (or 360¬∞).

Q1. (Basic) State the bearings of the following directions:
(a) Due East   (b) South-East   (c) North-West
(a) \( 090^\circ \) (b) \( 135^\circ \) (c) \( 315^\circ \)
Q2. (Back Bearing) The bearing of Town X from Town Y is \( 215^\circ \). Calculate the bearing of Town Y from Town X.
Subtract 180: \( 215 - 180 = 035^\circ \).
Q3. (Logic) A plane flies on a bearing of \( 120^\circ \). It then turns \( 90^\circ \) clockwise. What is the new bearing?
\( 120 + 90 = 210^\circ \).
Q4. (Geometry) In triangle ABC, Point A is due North of Point B. The bearing of C from B is \( 040^\circ \). The bearing of C from A is \( 110^\circ \).
Calculate the angle \( \angle BCA \).
North at B goes up to A. So line AB is North-South.
Bearing C from B = 040 implies \( \angle ABC = 40^\circ \).
Bearing C from A = 110. North at A is parallel to North at B.
Use geometry: \( \angle BAC = 180 - 110 = 70^\circ \).
Angle sum: \( 180 - (40+70) = 70^\circ \).
Q5. (Challenge) A ship sails from P to Q on a bearing of \( 070^\circ \). It then sails from Q to R on a bearing of \( 160^\circ \).
Calculate the angle \( \angle PQR \). (Hint: Sketch the North line at Q).
Back bearing of P from Q = \( 070 + 180 = 250^\circ \).
Bearing of R from Q = \( 160^\circ \).
Angle PQR = Difference = \( 250 - 160 = 90^\circ \).
