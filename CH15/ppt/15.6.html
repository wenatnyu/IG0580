2. The Sine Wave (Top Graph):

  • Starts at 0, peaks at 1 (\(90^\circ\)), drops to 0 (\(180^\circ\)).
  • Obtuse Angles (90¬∞-180¬∞): The graph is ABOVE the line. Sine is POSITIVE.
  • Rule: \( \sin(180 - x) = \sin(x) \).

3. The Cosine Wave (Bottom Graph):

  • Starts at 1, drops to 0 (\(90^\circ\)), bottoms at -1 (\(180^\circ\)).
  • Obtuse Angles (90¬∞-180¬∞): The graph is BELOW the line. Cosine is NEGATIVE.
  • Rule: \( \cos(180 - x) = - \cos(x) \).

Part 2: In-Class Examples

Ex 1. Without a calculator, write down the value of \( \sin(150^\circ) \).
Look at the graph symmetry: \( \sin(150) = \sin(180-150) = \sin(30) \).
We know \( \sin(30) = 0.5 \).
Ex 2. Solve \( \sin x = 0.5 \) for \( 0^\circ \le x \le 180^\circ \).
Calculator: \( x = 30^\circ \).
Graph Symmetry (180 - 30): \( x = 150^\circ \).
Answers: 30¬∞ and 150¬∞.

Q1. (Signs) State whether the following values are Positive or Negative (refer to the graphs):
(a) \( \sin 100^\circ \)    (b) \( \cos 100^\circ \)    (c) \( \tan 100^\circ \)
(a) Positive (Graph is above axis).
(b) Negative (Graph is below axis).
(c) Negative (Tan is sin/cos = pos/neg).
Q2. (Symmetry) Given that \( \sin 40^\circ \approx 0.643 \), find another angle between \( 0^\circ \) and \( 180^\circ \) that has the same sine value.
Using \( \sin(180-x) = \sin x \):
Angle = \( 180 - 40 = 140^\circ \).
Q3. (Exact Values) Without a calculator, find the exact value of \( \cos 120^\circ \).
Obtuse Cosine is negative.
\( \cos 120 = -\cos(180-120) = -\cos 60 \).
Exact value = -0.5.
Q4. (Solving Equations) Solve \( 2 \sin x = 1 \) for \( 0^\circ \le x \le 360^\circ \).
Isolate sin: \( \sin x = 0.5 \).
Ref angle = 30¬∞. Positive sin means Quad 1 & 2.
Solutions: 30¬∞ and 150¬∞.
