14.1 Simultaneous Linear Equations [3 marks]

1. Solve the simultaneous equations.

5x - 2y = 16
3x + 4y = 7

Answer: x = 3, y = -0.5
14.2 Linear Inequalities [3 marks]

2. Solve the inequality.

3(x - 2) < 5x + 4

Show your solution on a number line.

Answer: x > -5 (Open circle at -5, arrow to the right)
14.2 Linear Inequalities [3 marks]

3. Find the integer values of n that satisfy the inequality.

-7 ≤ 3n - 1 < 8

Answer: -2, -1, 0, 1, 2
14.3 Regions in a plane [3 marks]

4. By shading the unwanted regions of the grid, find and label the region R that satisfies the following three inequalities:

y ≥ 2
x ≥ 1
y ≤ -2x + 8

(Note: You can check if the point (2, 3) lies in this region.)

Answer: The region is a triangle with vertices at (1,2), (1,6), and (3,2). Point (2,3) is inside.
14.6 Factorising Quadratics (a ≠ 1) [2 marks]

5. Factorise completely.

2x2 + 7x - 4

Answer: (2x - 1)(x + 4)
14.6 Factorising Quadratics (a ≠ 1) [3 marks]

6. Factorise completely.

6x2 - 13x + 6

Answer: (2x - 3)(3x - 2)
14.4 Completing the Square [3 marks]

7. Write the expression x2 - 8x + 5 in the form (x + a)2 + b.

Answer: (x - 4)2 - 11
14.4 Completing the Square (Advanced) [4 marks]

8. Write 2x2 + 12x + 7 in the form a(x + b)2 + c.

Answer: 2(x + 3)2 - 11
14.5 Quadratic Formula [4 marks]

9. Solve the equation using the quadratic formula. Show all your working and give your answers correct to 2 decimal places.

3x2 - 2x - 7 = 0

Answer: x = 1.89 or x = -1.23
14.4 & 14.5 Solving Quadratics (Surds) [4 marks]

10. Solve the equation by completing the square or using the formula. Give your answers in exact form (using surds).

x2 + 6x - 2 = 0

Answer: x = -3 + √11 or x = -3 - √11
