Part 1: Algebra (Chapter 14)

Simultaneous Coefficient Region Eliminate Boundary Quadratic Numerator Denominator Factorise Inequality
Two linear equations that are solved together to find \(x\) and \(y\) are called Simultaneous equations.
In the algebraic fraction \( \frac{3x}{x+1} \), the bottom part \( (x+1) \) is called the Denominator.
The number appearing before a variable (e.g., the 5 in \( 5x^2 \)) is called the Coefficient.
To remove a variable from a system of equations, we use the method to Eliminate it.
The formula \( x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \) is used to solve Quadratic equations.
A shaded area on a graph satisfying a set of inequalities is called a Region.
The process of putting an expression back into brackets (e.g., \( x^2-9 \to (x-3)(x+3) \)) is to Factorise.
The top part of a fraction is known as the Numerator.
The line \( y = 2x + 1 \) can act as a Boundary for an inequality region.
A mathematical statement using symbols like \( <, >, \le, \ge \) is called an Inequality.

Part 2: Geometry & Trigonometry (Chapter 15)

Hypotenuse Bearing Clockwise Scale Elevation Depression Obtuse Tangent Adjacent Opposite
The longest side of a right-angled triangle is always the Hypotenuse.
A direction given as an angle measured from North is called a Bearing.
Bearings must always be measured in a Clockwise direction.
An angle that is greater than \( 90^\circ \) but less than \( 180^\circ \) is an Obtuse angle.
Looking up at an object from the horizontal creates an angle of Elevation.
Looking down at an object from a height creates an angle of Depression.
A map uses a Scale (e.g., 1 : 50000) to represent real-world distances.
The trigonometric ratio defined as \( \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}} \) is Tangent.
The side touching the angle (but not the hypotenuse) is the Adjacent side.
The side directly across from the angle in question is the Opposite side.
