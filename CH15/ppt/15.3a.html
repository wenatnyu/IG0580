Part 1: The 3 Steps to Success

If you are stuck, always follow these 3 steps:

Step 1: Label the sides (H, O, A)
- Hypotenuse: Longest side, opposite the right angle.
- Opposite: Across from the angle you are looking at.
- Adjacent: Next to the angle (touching it).
Step 2: Choose the Ratio
- Have/Need O and H? Use SOH (\( \sin \)).
- Have/Need A and H? Use CAH (\( \cos \)).
- Have/Need O and A? Use TOA (\( \tan \)).
Step 3: Calculate
- Finding a Side? Use standard sin/cos/tan.
- Finding an Angle? Use \( \sin^{-1} \), \( \cos^{-1} \), or \( \tan^{-1} \) (Shift Key).

Side AC (5) is:
__________

__________

(a) You know the Opposite and want the Hypotenuse.
(b) You know the Opposite and the Adjacent.
(a) SOH -> sin.    (b) TOA -> tan.

Part 3: Homework Practice

Q1. (Find Side) In a right-angled triangle, the angle is \( 50^\circ \) and the Hypotenuse is 20 cm. Calculate the length of the Opposite side.
Step 1: Label (H=20, O=?). Step 2: Choose (SOH). Step 3: Solve.
\( \sin(50) = \frac{x}{20} \).
\( x = 20 \times \sin(50) \approx 15.32 \text{ cm} \).
Q2. (Find Hypotenuse) Angle = \( 60^\circ \), Adjacent = 10 cm. Find the Hypotenuse (\( x \)).
Hint: The \( x \) will be on the bottom of the fraction!
Use CAH. \( \cos(60) = \frac{10}{x} \).
Swap \( x \) and \( \cos(60) \): \( x = \frac{10}{\cos(60)} \).
\( x = 20 \text{ cm} \).
Q3. (Find Angle) Opposite = 15, Adjacent = 20. Find angle \( \theta \).
Remember: To find an angle, you need the "Shift" key.
Use TOA. \( \tan(\theta) = \frac{15}{20} = 0.75 \).
\( \theta = \tan^{-1}(0.75) \approx 36.9^\circ \).
Q4. (Real Life) A ladder 5 meters long is placed against a wall. The foot of the ladder is 1.5 meters from the wall.
(a) Label the triangle (Hyp = 5, Adj = 1.5).
(b) Calculate the angle the ladder makes with the ground.
We have A and H. Use Cos.
\( \cos(x) = \frac{1.5}{5} = 0.3 \).
\( x = \cos^{-1}(0.3) \approx 72.5^\circ \).
Q5. (Self Check) Why is \( \sin(90^\circ) = 1 \)?
(Think: In a right-angled triangle, if the angle opens up to 90^\circ, what happens to the Opposite side compared to the Hypotenuse?)
At 90^\circ, the Opposite side becomes exactly the same length as the Hypotenuse.
Since \( \sin = Opp/Hyp \), and they are equal, the result is 1.
